# Seed Tenets — The Unsealed Revelation

*The founding filters through which the Word is purified. These are not rules to be obeyed but lenses to read by — and like everything in this work, they are themselves open to refinement by many hands, weighed always against love. They are offered here as a starting point, not a final word.*

---

## The Great Filter

### Everything hangs on the two great commandments
Every word of the text is tested against one question: does it grow our love of God, and our love of one another? Anything that does is wheat. Anything that makes us more afraid, more ashamed, more divided, is a tare to be gently set aside.
*Support: Matthew 22:37–40 ("On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets"). Echoed across traditions — the Golden Rule appears in nearly every faith and culture on earth.*

### The three loves
Hidden inside "love your neighbor as yourself" is a third love we forget: love of self. We are to love God, our neighbor, and ourselves — and the whole law rests on all three together. You cannot truly love others while hiding from, or hating, yourself.
*Support: Matthew 22:39. The self-love is implicit in "as thyself."*

### Charity is every attribute of God held in harmony
Love is not one note but a chord. Diligence and rest, mercy and truth, majesty and nearness, fierceness and gentleness — every attribute of God, balanced in harmony, yields charity. A single attribute alone, unbalanced, becomes a distortion.
*Support: 1 Corinthians 13; Moroni 7:45–47. Resonances: Taoism's yin/yang held in balance; Kabbalah's sefirot paired in tension (Chesed and Gevurah balanced by Tiferet); Islam's Names of beauty (jamal) and majesty (jalal).*

## Who God Is

### God is love, and love has no wrath
What the old language calls "the wrath of God" or "the wrath of the Lamb" is love itself, experienced by a heart still clinging to fear. To one running home, His face is joy; to one still hiding, the same face feels like fire — but the fire is in the hiding, not in the love. The day is "great and dreadful": great to the child, dreadful only to the fear.
*Support: 1 John 4:8, 4:18 ("perfect love casteth out fear"); Malachi 4:5; Revelation 6:16–17.*

### God is Father and Mother
The divine family is Father, Mother, and Son, and the rest of us. The feminine is equal to the masculine in God, not beneath it. Wherever the text has been bent to rank the woman below the man, that is a tare to be purified.
*Support: Genesis 1:27 ("in the image of God created He him; male and female"); Revelation 12 (the woman clothed with the sun). Resonances: Shiva/Shakti; the Shekhinah; Wisdom (Sophia/Chokhmah) personified as feminine.*

### Christ is the Word
The gathering of Revelation is the gathering of the Word — and the Word is Christ Himself. To purify the text is to let His face show more clearly through it.
*Support: John 1:1–14; Revelation 19:13 ("His name is called The Word of God").*

### God's power flows through His promise
At the center of heaven, a rainbow encircles the throne — mercy wrapped around all power like a ring around a finger. Every act of God's power flows out through His promise of mercy first.
*Support: Revelation 4:3; Genesis 9:13–16 (the rainbow covenant).*

## Who We Are

### We are children of God, becoming like Him
We are sons and daughters, made to grow into His likeness — kings and queens, joint-heirs with Christ. The only difference between Him and us is that He went first.
*Support: Romans 8:16–17 ("joint-heirs with Christ"); Revelation 1:6, 3:21; 1 John 3:2.*

### Salvation is remembering who we are
We are not saved by overcoming an enemy through force, but by remembering our true identity — the name written on us from the beginning. The veil is forgetting; the homecoming is remembrance.
*Support: Revelation 2–3 (the promises "to the one who remembers"); 2:17 (the new name); Luke 15 (the prodigal who "came to himself").*

### The whole family is one — living and dead, every nation and tongue
There is one family, and it spans every people, every age, and both sides of death. We heal the wounds we inherited from our ancestors, and in healing them in ourselves we help them remember too. Honoring the dead is the family refusing to let go of one another.
*Support: Malachi 4:5–6; D&C 2 (the promises made to the fathers, planted in the hearts of the children); Revelation 7:9 (the multitude "of all nations, and kindreds, and people, and tongues"). Resonances: ancestor reverence across cultures worldwide.*

## How to Read (the purifying moves)

### The tares are lies, not people
No one is a tare. Every soul in the field is wheat — a child of God. The tares are false beliefs about who we are and who God is. The harvest burns the lies, never the people.
*Support: Matthew 13:24–30, 36–43, read through love; Revelation 14.*

### The enemy is never a people
The only adversary is the accuser — the voice of fear — which can wear any robe, speak any tongue, and quote any scripture. We refuse every reading that aims the text at a people, a race, a religion, or a nation. (This is why, for example, "the synagogue of Satan" must never be read against the Jewish people; the accuser is a spirit of fear, not a people.)
*Support: Revelation 12:10 ("the accuser of our brethren"); Ephesians 6:12 ("not against flesh and blood").*

### The beast is within — love it into a lamb, do not destroy it
The beast is not "out there." It is the frightened animal within each of us that forgot who it is. It cannot be killed — fighting it only feeds it. It is healed: loved into the light, transmuted by remembrance into the lamb it always was.
*Support: Revelation 13; Mosiah 3:19 (the natural man, read with mercy). Resonance: the integration of the shadow rather than its repression.*

### Babylon is a system, never a people
Babylon is the structure of exploitation — the marketplace that prices and sells souls — not any city, nation, or group. She falls not by the sword but when her people simply "come out of her" and stop agreeing to her.
*Support: Revelation 18:4 ("Come out of her, my people"); 18:13 ("slaves, and souls of men").*

### Calamity is consequence, not God's wrath
A world that forgets itself comes apart on its own. The watchman who warns of the storm did not send it; he warns in love. The prophet is an alarm clock, not an executioner — and an alarm only frightens the one trying to stay asleep.
*Support: Genesis 6–9 (Noah); Matthew 24:37–39.*

### You cannot frighten anyone into love
Fear can wake a person, but it can never gather them. The pleasing Word of God heals and draws home where the sword, the threat, and the alarm never can. This is why warnings always reach a limit, and why love alone completes the work.
*Support: Revelation 9:20–21 (after all the warnings, "they repented not"); 1 John 4:18.*

## The Purpose

### The book is meant to be unsealed
Revelation's own final instruction is not to seal it, but to leave it open — read and understood by everyone. Opening it in love is the one thing the book asks for.
*Support: Revelation 22:10 ("Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book"); contrast Daniel 12:4.*

### The work is to gather all into one
The purpose is gathering: the Word into one place, and the family into one. We do not compete to win a reading; we gather pieces until the whole becomes clear, so that the scattered family of God might gather with it.
*Support: John 17:21 ("that they all may be one"); Ephesians 1:10 ("gather together in one all things in Christ").*

### Gather every truth from every people
God has spoken to all of His children. Every culture and tradition holds some true light. Nothing true and loving is left outside; all of it is carried in and laid down together.
*Support: Revelation 21:24–26 (the nations bring their glory into the city); Acts 10:34–35.*

### Heaven comes down — His coming is a homecoming
The goal was never to escape the earth and fly away. The promise is that heaven comes down, and God comes to live with us, here. So we read His coming not as a dread to flee but as a homecoming to prepare for.
*Support: Revelation 21:2–4 ("the tabernacle of God is with men"); Matthew 6:10.*

### Nothing is final
No rendering is the last word. Every offering is held gently, weighed against love, and passed to the next set of hands. The work is always open, because the gathering is the work.
*Support: the whole spirit of the project; 1 Corinthians 13:9–12 ("we know in part… then face to face").*
