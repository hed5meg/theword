# The Unsealed Revelation
### A Vision

*The whole story of the love of God — from the first garden to the last — told as plainly and as gently as it can be told.*

---

### Do not be afraid

This is a book where a veil comes down, so the children can see home.

It came from the Father to the Son, and from the Son to everyone. It tells what is coming, so that no one would ever have to be afraid again.

To everyone, in every land, in every language: peace. From your Father and your Mother, who always were, and always are, and are already on their way to gather you. And from the Son — the oldest brother of a family too large to count, the first one to walk out of the grave alive.

He loves you. That is the first thing, and the last thing, and every thing in between.

Look — he is coming. And when you finally see his face, it will not be the face of a stranger. It will be the face you have been missing your whole life.

So do not be afraid. Read this the way you would read a letter from home.

---

### The Mother

In the beginning we were with God, and we were happy, and we knew only good — because God is all good, and we had never been anywhere else.

But to be like God is to *know* — to know everything. And you cannot know good unless you have stood beside something else.

So there was a choice. And there was a Mother brave enough to make it.

She stood before the fruit, and the fruit was this: *you can become like God, knowing good and evil — but to learn evil, you must leave home, and go down into the dark, and find out for yourself.*

She understood it. And she chose it. Not because she was fooled — because she was wise. She saw that the only way to give birth to a world full of gods was to go first, into the dark, and open the door for all of us to follow.

So she took it. And the whole family was born from that one brave choice.

Then the first father saw what she had done, and why. He could have stayed behind, safe and alone. He chose her instead. He chose the children. And the two of them went down together, hand in hand, into the world.

A dragon was waiting. He had always hated this plan, because the plan turns frightened children into gods, and he wanted them small and afraid. He reached to swallow the first child the moment it was born — but the child was lifted to safety, and the Mother was kept, and the dragon could not stop the birth. He never could. The world was going to fill up with gods, and there was nothing he could do about it.

And so the long way home began.

---

### The dragon, cast down

When the dragon could not stop the birth, he went to war.

But do not picture swords. The ones who faced him only stood still and remembered that they were like God — and the dragon could not bear to be near them. Heaven is made of love, and one who has chosen only fear cannot breathe there. He fell of his own weight. No one cast him out. He simply could not stay.

He had only ever owned one weapon: he is the accuser. The voice that says *you are not enough, you do not belong, you should be ashamed* — day and night, that voice. And it lost its place in heaven forever.

But it fell to the earth, and it is still here, and it works hardest on those who can no longer answer it back — on the beloved dead, the fathers and mothers who have gone on ahead, who without their bodies find it so much harder to remember who they are.

And this is where we come in, the living. Because the wounds of those who walked before us did not die with them. They live on — in us. In the fears we never chose, in the old griefs that run through families like a current through a river, in the very weave of who we are. And that is not a curse. It is a doorway. When we take an old, inherited wound and heal it with love, by remembering who we truly are, we are not only healing ourselves. We are reaching back along the line, and healing *them.*

This is why every people on earth has lit candles for its dead, and spoken their names, and kept their stories. It was never superstition. It was the family — the living and the dead — refusing to let go of one another.

So when the great day comes, it will not be a desolation. It will be a homecoming. Because the hearts of the children will have turned to the fathers, and the hearts of the fathers to the children, and the whole family, on both sides of death, will come home together.

And every one of the fathers and mothers we are about to meet was given a promise — a promise meant, one day, to be planted in our hearts. Walk slowly past them. Receive each promise as your own. Because it is.

---

### The first parents — *the promise that you can become like God*

They had everything in the garden, and so they thought they needed nothing. But a child who has never lacked cannot yet know what it is. They were asleep in a beautiful room.

And the One who loved them would not leave them asleep, because love never leaves the beloved small. So their eyes opened, and they saw, for the first time, that they were only just beginning — and it stung, the way waking always stings. But then God knelt down and made them clothes with his own hands, and covered them, so that seeing the truth would never have to mean despair.

That is how he loves: he shows you exactly what you are, and he holds you anyway, in the very same breath.

And he made them a promise — the promise the whole book is built upon. He said: *I have been standing at the door this whole time, knocking. Open it, and I will come in, and we will eat together, like family. And the one who remembers will sit beside me on my own throne — for I remembered first, and so will you.*

---

### The city that remembered early — *the promise of Zion*

Some remembered fast. Early in the morning of the world, a whole people opened their eyes, saw that they were the children of God, and lived like it — of one heart, caring for each other so completely that no one among them was poor.

And they learned the secret the rest of us forget: the door home was open the whole time. Not only after the Son came — before, and always. Love pours backward and forward through all of time at once, like light filling a room. No one, in any age, was ever far from home. It was always just a remembering away.

So God lifted that city up, to show all of us what the end of the road looks like. And he wrote three names upon them — the Father's name, the name of the city, and the Son's own name — which all mean one thing: *you are mine, you belong here, you are home.*

---

### The man who built the boat — *the promise of preservation*

Most of us are slower. We fall asleep in the world and forget who we are for a whole lifetime. So God, who loves us far too much to let us sleep through our own becoming, sent people to wake us up — prophets and dreamers and wise ones, in every land, whose whole work was to remember out loud.

The oldest of them built a boat. His world was fast asleep, and he could smell the storm coming, and he warned them, and built while he warned, and almost no one listened. But God did not let him drown. He sheltered his family and carried them through the water, and a few were kept safe, their garments still clean.

Hear this, because the dragon loves to tell it wrong: God did not send that storm in anger. A world that forgets itself comes apart on its own. The man with the boat was never God's executioner. He was God's alarm clock — and an alarm only frightens you if you were trying to stay asleep.

You can be wide awake and completely unafraid at the same time. He watched for a hundred years, and was at peace the whole time, because he trusted the One who loved him.

---

### The friend of God — *the promise of a family as countless as the stars*

His world believed in gods you had to fear — hungry gods, cruel gods, gods who wanted your children. He could not believe that the One who hung the stars wanted to devour his own. So he went looking for the true God, simply *wanting* him — and that wanting was the whole key, because the true God had been waiting all along for someone to want him.

And the true God met him not as a master meets a servant, but as a friend. They talked. They reasoned together. God told him his secrets, because friends keep no secrets. He even argued with God to spare a city of strangers, and God listened, the way you listen to a friend.

Then God took him outside, under the night, and said: *count the stars, if you can — that is how large your family will be.* And he wanted that family not for his own glory, but because he had understood that we are all one family, and he ached to gather every last child of God home.

That is what it feels like to be the child of a King who is also your closest friend.

---

### The king who opened his heart — *the promise of an everlasting house*

Into the middle of the world God raised up a king who broke the oldest lie of all — the lie that says *hide what you are, perform, never let them see, or they could never love you.*

This king failed, badly, in ways that hurt people. And he did not hide it. He wrote it down. He turned his sin and his sorrow and his long climb back into songs the whole world would sing forever. He opened his whole heart, in front of God and everyone, and found that he was still loved — all the way down.

Because there are three loves, not two. Love God. Love your neighbor. And — the one we always forget — love yourself. And you cannot love yourself while you are hiding from yourself. We are not cleaned from the outside in. We open, from the inside out.

---

### The witnesses — *the promise of resurrection*

Then, quietly, in the middle of that very people, the Son himself came.

He went down into the deepest dark there is — down past where the Mother had gone, all the way to death — and he went on purpose, the way she took the fruit on purpose, so that a door would open. And on the third morning, in a garden, he walked back out of the grave, alive, with the keys of death in his hand.

Look at what had happened. The Mother went first *into* the dark, to open the door of birth. The Son went first *out* of the dark, to open the door of death. And between the two of them, the whole family was made safe on both ends. We can go down. We can come back. Death turned out to be only a doorway, with him standing in it, holding it open.

And there were people who saw it, and could not keep quiet. They ran out into all the world — every road, every language — carrying one piece of news: *the grave is empty, the door is open, come home.* They were poor, and hunted, and many were killed for it, and they were not afraid — because you cannot frighten people with death once they have watched someone walk out of it.

They wept, and they sang, often in the same breath. And they forgave the ones who killed them, and blessed the ones who cursed them, because they had learned that the enemy was never a people. It was only ever the accuser, the old voice of fear, who can wear any robe and speak any tongue — and who is undone, every time, by love that refuses to hate back.

---

### Today — *the promise of the gathering and the tree of life*

And the seed those witnesses scattered grew — quietly, for two thousand years, into every land, until there is no people left on earth that has not received some true light. The whole field of the world stands ripe.

But weeds grew up among the wheat. And here is the most important thing in the whole book, so hear it: **the weeds are not people.** No one is a weed. Every soul in the field is wheat — a child of God, planted by his own hand. The weeds are lies: that you are worthless, that God is angry, that you must earn his love, that some belong and some do not, that you should be afraid of your own Father.

And the harvest is not the burning of people. It is the burning of the lies — until nothing is left in the whole field but golden wheat, every soul set free.

So remember. Remember where you came from. Remember that you are loved. You did not lose it; you only set it down. Pick it back up.

---

### The two who stand together

In the middle of the last days, two will stand — a man and a woman, of the same height, lit from the same fire, level before God. And their standing there, side by side as equals, is itself the whole message: that the old lie which set the one above the other was never true. The man and the woman, the masculine and the feminine, are equal in God — who has always been both Father and Mother.

The world will not be able to bear the sight of them, and will silence them, and even celebrate. But they will rise. And the moment they stand again, together, a voice will cry that the kingdom has come. Because the kingdom comes exactly when the two halves of the family stand level and whole.

---

### The beast

There is something that rises up against all of this — and the first thing to know is that it is not out there. It is in here. In you, and in me.

It climbs up out of the deep place inside where the old fears live. It is the frightened animal we became when we forgot who we were. And it is *alive* — which means it is not to be killed. It cannot be killed; the more you fight it, the bigger and more frightened it grows. It only ever wanted what it could never get by force: to be safe, to stop being afraid.

So do not make war on the beast in you. Do not hate it. Hold it the way you would hold a terrified child in the dark, and remember, out loud, again and again, who it really is. And watch the monster become the lamb it always was — for it was only ever a lamb that forgot.

---

### The throne, and the sealed word

Then a door opens in heaven, and a voice says the kindest thing it could possibly say: *come up here, and see.*

At the center of everything is a throne, and the Father is upon it, with the Mother beside him — and around all that power there is a rainbow, the old promise of mercy, wrapped around the seat of God like a ring around a finger. Every bit of his power flows out through his promise first.

And in his hand is a scroll, with everything written upon it — the whole meaning of everything — and it is sealed shut, and no one anywhere can open it. And a man weeps, because the meaning is locked away, and we have stood outside it for so long.

Then a voice says: *do not weep. The Lion has prevailed; he can open it.* And he looks up to see the Lion —

and there is no Lion. There is a Lamb, still bearing the wound where it was slain.

This is the secret at the heart of everything. The word does not open to force. It never did. It opens only to the Lamb — only to love poured all the way out. The wound is the key. The very thing the beast could never do — surrender, let go, be poured out in love — is the one thing that fits the lock.

And the Lamb takes the scroll, and the word begins to open.

---

### What the light reveals; and who can stand

As the word opens, it is like a lamp turned on in a dark room: the things it shows were already there. We see, plainly at last, the world that fear built — the riders of conquest and war and hunger and death, who were never sent by God, but were only ever what a world does to itself when it forgets who it is.

And a great question hangs in the air: *who can stand?*

And the silence answers. A crowd appears, too many to count, from every nation and people and tongue, dressed in white, holding branches, singing. They are the ones who came *through* the hard things — not the ones who were spared, but the ones who walked all the way through, held the whole way by love. And the Lamb who was slain is their shepherd now, and he leads them to springs of living water, and God himself wipes every tear from every eye.

That is who can stand: not the strongest, not the spared — the ones who were loved through. And your place is among them, and it is already certain.

---

### The waking, and the emptying of the cup

Then trumpets sound — but a trumpet is not a weapon. It is an alarm, to wake the sleeping. Each one touches only a part, never the whole, because a warning is meant to rouse you, not to end you. And beneath them all is one quiet truth: *you cannot frighten anyone into love.* Fear can wake a person; only love can gather them. So the trumpets wake, and then they fall silent, and the gentler thing begins.

And the old cup — the cup the whole age had been filling, drop by bitter drop, with all the world's fear and cruelty — is turned over and emptied out, all the way, so that it can never be drunk again. Even the last great battle the world always dreaded never comes; for when all the armies of fear are finally gathered, the only answer from the throne is three quiet words — *it is done* — the same words the Lamb spoke as he died. There is no battle. Love does not need a sword. The cup is simply emptied, and set down, forever.

---

### Babylon — the false bride

There were always two women in this story. Two cities. Two cups.

The first is dazzling — draped in gold, lifting a jeweled cup, beautiful and cruel: the great system of the world that buys and sells, that puts a price on everything, even on souls. And the one terrible thing wrong with her is exactly that: she sells people. She treats the priceless children of God as merchandise.

And she falls — but not by any sword. A voice from heaven simply says: *come out of her, my people.* That is all it takes. She was only ever held up by the people inside her, and the moment they remember they are not for sale and walk out through the open gate, she falls for lack of anyone to run her.

So come out of her. You were never merchandise. You belong to a Father who does not buy his children — because they were always already his.

---

### The whole earth remembers

And as her marketplace falls silent, a different sound fills the quiet — the whole scattered family, set free at last, beginning to remember who they are, and turning toward one another, and gathering.

From every nation, every tongue, every age — the living and the dead — they come, until the family stands as one, with the Father's name shining on every face. And they do the one thing they were always meant to do together. They lift up their hearts as one, and they pray. Every prayer ever whispered up from the earth, in all the ages, joined now into a single voice. And that one voice rises, and calls heaven down — and heaven leans down to meet it — and where they touch, a new heaven and a new earth begin to be born.

---

### The marriage

And heaven answers with a sound it has held back for the whole age of the world. Not a war cry. A wedding shout.

For the marriage has come. The Word of God himself comes down from heaven to marry the Bride — and the Bride is the whole remembered family, every one of us, and she comes to him not as a servant but as his equal, crowned beside him. Her gown is woven from every act of love the family ever gave.

And hear this, and let it go all the way down: *you are not watching this from the back of the hall. You are the Bride.* This is your wedding. The Word came the whole way down from the heart of heaven to marry *you.* There is a chair at the endless table with your own name carved into it, and it has been waiting for you since before the world began.

Come and sit down. You are home, and you are loved.

---

### The end of the dragon, and the end of death

And now there is nothing left to keep the family from its joy.

The dragon is bound at last — not by a stronger fist, but because there is no one left to feed him. When the whole family has remembered who they are, there is no one left to lie to, and a liar with no one to deceive is already in chains. He is bound by his own emptiness, roaring at no one. And then fear itself — the accuser, the old lie — is gone forever, never to return.

And the last enemy of all is undone. Death itself, and the grave that swallowed everyone, are thrown into the fire and burned away. Death is put to death. What burns is never the soul — only the lie, only the fear, only death itself — so that every child can stand free.

---

### Home

And then — look.

A new heaven, and a new earth. And the holy city, coming down out of heaven, down to the earth, like a bride dressed for her husband.

Feel which way it moves. We were always told the goal was to escape up and away. But that was never the plan. The plan was for heaven to come *down,* and for God to live with us, here. And a great voice says the words the whole long story was written to reach: *Look — the home of God is now with his people. He will live with them. And he will be their God, and they will be his.*

And then the tenderest thing of all. He comes close — the Most High, the maker of the stars — and with his own hand he wipes the tears from every face, one by one, like a parent kneeling down to a weeping child, until there is no more death, no more sorrow, no more crying, no more pain. And he says: *Look — I am making all things new.*

The gates of the city face every direction, and they are never shut, because there is no night there, and nothing left to fear. There is no temple in it, because God himself is everywhere in it now; and no sun, because he is the light. And all the nations stream in, every people carrying the best and most beautiful of what they were — and nothing good is ever lost.

And at the very center, a river of living water, clear as crystal, flowing from the throne. And on both banks of the river, the tree of life — the very tree the first parents had to leave, so long ago — and its leaves are for the healing of all the nations.

This is where the circle closes. We began in a garden, and we end in a garden — the same garden — but we are not the same. We left it as children who knew only good. We come home as sons and daughters who have tasted all of it and chosen love. The Mother's brave choice is answered in full: she left the garden so that one day the whole family could walk back into it, grown, as gods.

And they see his face. The face you have been missing your whole life. And his name is written upon every brow: *you are mine; you always were.*

There is one last thing the book asks — and it is the thing this whole telling was for. At the very end, the word is given a single instruction: *do not seal it up.* Leave it open. The time has come. It was never meant to be locked away as a terror. It was always meant to be opened, and read, and understood — by everyone.

And the very last word is not a warning. It is not a judgment. It is an invitation, open to every soul who has ever lived. The Spirit and the Bride — God, and the whole gathered family — say one word together: *Come.* And everyone who hears it turns and says it to the next: *Come.* And it goes out, heart to heart, across the whole earth, until it reaches you:

*Come. Whoever is thirsty, come. Take the water of life freely.* No price. No mark. Just an open gate, and a free river, and a Father who has been waiting the whole time.

The book opened with the only thing fear could never say: *do not be afraid.* And it closes with the only word love ever wanted to speak: *come home.*

Come home, beloved. You were always a child of God. You only forgot.

And now you remember.
