We believe the book of Revelation was never meant to stay sealed. Its own last pages ask that it be left open — read, and understood, by everyone. For we believe the coming of our Savior waits on a single thing: that we come to understand who He truly is, and in the same moment come to understand who we truly are — beloved children of God, made to grow into His likeness. He cannot fully come to a people who do not yet know Him, or know themselves. And so the unsealing of this book is not a side errand; it is preparation for His return.
Because Revelation, read in its pure form, is exactly that story — the story of God's interaction with His children on the earth, from the first garden to the last. It is the whole human family's journey home, and it shows us His face and our own at once. We believe it will be opened not by one scholar or one church, but the way the pure Word of God has always been recovered: by the desire of His children, gathered together, uniting in prayer, and filtering every word through love until what is dark becomes plain, pure, and precious.
This is the work we are inviting you into, and the idea is simple. Each of us carries a piece of the truth — shaped by our culture, our language, our tradition, our hard-won understanding of God. Alone, none of us holds the whole. But gathered into one place, tested against the two great commandments — to love God, and to love one another as ourselves — the pieces begin to fit, the lies fall away like chaff, and the Word grows clearer than any of us could make it alone. We are not trying to win an argument about Revelation. We are gathering its true meaning into one place, so that the scattered family of God might gather with it.
So this is an open table. Bring your own rendering of Revelation in its purest form. Bring the principles you filter it through — the lenses you trust to tell wheat from tares. Bring your questions, your corrections, your tradition's wisdom, your mother tongue. Whatever helps love and truth come through more clearly belongs here. Nothing is final, and no one holds the pen alone; every offering is held gently, weighed against love, and refined by the next set of hands.
If your heart turns toward this — if you long to know Him as He truly is, to remember who you are, and to see the whole human family gathered home into one — then you are already one of us. Come and add your light. The field is wide, the work is joyful, and there is a place at the table with your name on it.
